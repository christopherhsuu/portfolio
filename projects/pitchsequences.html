Pitch Sequences

An interactive visualization of pitch sequences, pitch types, and swing outcomes. The app helps explore sequencing patterns and batter responses by zone and count.

Launch (Live) Code

Overview (for non-experts)

This project shows the sequence of pitches thrown in an at-bat and the result of each pitch (swing, miss, contact). It's like replaying the order of pitches so you can see patterns and how batters react.

Who it's for

Coaches and fans who want to understand how pitch order, location, and type change a batter's chances to swing or make contact.

Quick start

  1. Launch the demo.
  2. Search for a pitcher or batter, then pick a game or date range.
  3. Hover individual pitches to view details (pitch type, speed, result) and use filters to compare situations.

Inputs & Outputs

Inputs: pitch-by-pitch event data (location, type, result). Outputs: interactive sequence plots, aggregated summaries by count and zone, and exportable CSVs for further analysis.

Methodology (technical)

I parsed pitch-by-pitch data from Statcast, normalized pitch locations, and labeled events (swing, miss, contact). I built a sequence visualization with interactive filters (player, pitch type, count) and aggregated summaries. Visualization is implemented with D3 inside Streamlit for interactivity.
