Pitch Sequences

An interactive visualization of pitch sequences, pitch types, and swing outcomes. The app helps explore sequencing patterns and batter responses by zone and count.

Launch Pitch Sequences (Live) Code

Methodology

I parsed pitch-by-pitch data from Statcast, normalized pitch locations, and labeled events (swing, miss, contact). I built a sequence visualization with interactive filters (player, pitch type, count) and aggregated summaries.

Findings

Common sequencing patterns emerge by pitcher and matchup: effective pitchers use change-of-pace pitches to disrupt timing, while location diversity correlates with lower slugging against. Visual exploration helped identify several patterns worth deeper modeling.

How I built it

  1. Data ingest: Statcast CSVs via the MLB API.
  2. Cleaning: normalized coordinates, removed incomplete pitches, and merged play-level context.
  3. Visualization: D3 for custom pitch maps inside a Streamlit app.
  4. Export: packaged as a Streamlit app and deployed to Streamlit Cloud.
